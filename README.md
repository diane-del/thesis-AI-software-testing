##  Abstract (Short Version)

Artificial Intelligence is reshaping software testing by enabling smarter automation, predictive techniques, and self-healing mechanisms. Through four case studies (Safran, Schneider Electric, Sparco, and Opencell) and a survey of industry professionals, this thesis evaluates the concrete benefits of AI in testingâ€”reduced time-to-market, higher coverage, improved reliabilityâ€”while also addressing challenges such as data governance, model drift, lack of skills, opacity, and organisational resistance.

The findings highlight that AI does not replace human testers; instead, it augments their analytical capabilities. Successful adoption depends on three pillars: **technical mastery, strong data governance, and organisational maturity**. Recommendations include phased adoption, hybrid AIâ€“rules approaches, investment in skills, and responsible AI governance aligned with European standards.
